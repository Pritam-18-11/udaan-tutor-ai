📖 What does this show?

Each ball is dropped simultaneously on 5 different celestial bodies. The ball on the Sun hits the ground first because the Sun has the strongest gravitational pull (274 m/s²). The Moon ball lands last with just 1.62 m/s². This visually demonstrates Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation — the greater the mass, the stronger the gravity.

🌌 The Solar System

Our Solar System has the Sun at the center and 8 planets orbiting due to gravitational force. Planets closer to the Sun (Mercury, Venus) orbit faster due to stronger gravity. Distant planets like Neptune take much longer — 165 Earth years! All orbits are elliptical as described by Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion.
